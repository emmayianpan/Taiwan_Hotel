Market Overview
The section is analyzed and visualized by utilizing hotel database from Tourism Bureau, Ministry of Transportation and Communications in Taiwan, with a focus on the data of 2019 and 2020. Based on the given data, supply and demand in Taiwan market were stable (achieve 500k+ every month) in 2019; however, the demand began to drop in January 2020 because of Covid-19 pandemic. Taiwan was one the few countries which took action on implementing travel restrictions and strict 14-day quarantine for people from aboard. Hence, the hotel market was suffered for months. In April 2020, as the Covid-19 situation was controlled nationwide, and people were more confident to go out again, the national demand increased (especially on leisure segment). Most of the hotels aimed to optimized occupancy primary to maximize RevPAR; therefore, ADR was decreased as expected. Overall, demand is anticipated to go up once Covid-19 is controlled worldwide, and Travel Bubble between Taiwan and other countries is opened. While demand goes up again, ADR is expected to be increased as well.
